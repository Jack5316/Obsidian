# Guardians of the Galaxy Vol. 2

## Metadata
- Runtime: 2h 16m
- Release year: 2017
- Director: James Gunn
- Cast: Chris Pratt as Peter Quill / Star-Lord, Zoe Saldaña as Gamora, Dave Bautista as Drax the Destroyer, Bradley Cooper as Rocket (voice), Vin Diesel as Baby Groot (voice), Karen Gillan as Nebula, Michael Rooker as Yondu Udonta, Kurt Russell as Ego
- Genre(s): Action, Adventure, Comedy

## Plot
The Guardians struggle to keep together as a team while dealing with their personal family issues, notably Star-Lord's encounter with his father, the ambitious celestial being Ego. After fleeing a Sovereign fleet following Rocket's theft of their valuable batteries, the team is rescued by Ego, who reveals himself as Peter's long-lost father and invites them to his home planet. As Peter explores his celestial heritage, the rest of the Guardians uncover Ego's dark, universe-altering ambitions—forcing the team to confront fractured loyalties, unresolved sibling feuds (between Gamora and Nebula), and the true meaning of family beyond blood ties. The narrative splits into parallel threads: Peter's journey with Ego, Rocket and Yondu's misadventures evading the Sovereign and a mutinous Ravager crew, and the gradual unspooling of Ego's idyllic facade.

## Interesting Points
- **Themes**: Central focus on chosen family vs. biological family, with Yondu and the Ravagers framing the film's emotional core; also explores grief, redemption, and the danger of godlike ambition (via Ego's plan to terraform the universe).
- **Craft**: James Gunn's signature needle-drops (featuring 1970s/80s pop deep cuts from *Awesome Mix Vol. 2*) anchor emotional beats; vibrant, psychedelic visual design for Ego's planet, with practical effects and prosthetics balancing the film's CGI; bold color grading that leans into retro sci-fi pastels.
- **Cultural Impact**: Solidified the Guardians as a cornerstone of the [[Marvel Cinematic Universe]]; elevated Yondu from a side character to a fan-favorite emotional anchor; popularized Baby Groot as a merchandising and cultural icon.
- **Connections**: Builds on the first film's team dynamic while deepening ties to MCU cosmic lore (teasing Celestials as a larger force); mirrors James Gunn's exploration of misfit families in his earlier work *Super*.
